\subsection*{Historical Repair Chain}

Each integral in this chapter changes one structural feature of the previous
one. The point is not to collect competing definitions, but to make the
failure of one definition produce the next repair.

\[
\begin{array}{c|c|c|c}

\subsection*{Chapter Handoff}

This chapter ends with two conclusions. First, Riemann and Darboux
integrability are controlled exactly by the size of the discontinuity set.
Second, gauge sums split into two successors: Henstock--Kurzweil preserves
conditional one-dimensional cancellation, while McShane recovers the Lebesgue
class. The next volume supplies the measure theory needed to make that
Lebesgue class intrinsic rather than anticipated.
