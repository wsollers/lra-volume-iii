\begin{corollary*}[Archimedean Reciprocal Corollary]
For every $x>0$ and every $y\in\mathbb{R}$, there exists $n\in\mathbb{N}$ such that $nx>y$.
\end{corollary*}

\begin{proof}[Professional Standard Proof]
\LRAProofBodyStart
Let $x>0$ and let $y\in\mathbb{R}$. Since $x$ is positive, the quotient
$y/x$ is a real number. By the Archimedean Property, there exists
$n\in\mathbb{N}$ such that
\[
  n>\frac{y}{x}.
\]
Multiplying both sides by the positive number $x$ preserves the inequality, so
\[
  nx>\frac{y}{x}x=y.
\]
Thus there exists $n\in\mathbb{N}$ such that $nx>y$.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Detailed Learning Proof]
\LRAProofBodyStart
Fix a positive real number $x$ and an arbitrary real number $y$. We need to
show that some natural-number multiple of the step size $x$ eventually exceeds
$y$.

Because $x>0$, division by $x$ is valid and produces the real number $y/x$.
The Archimedean Property applies to every real number, so it applies to
$y/x$. Hence there is a natural number $n$ with
\[
  n>\frac{y}{x}.
\]

The inequality direction is preserved when multiplying by $x$, because $x$ is
positive. Therefore
\[
  nx>\left(\frac{y}{x}\right)x.
\]
Since $x\ne 0$, the right-hand side simplifies to $y$. Hence $nx>y$, which is
exactly the required conclusion.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark*}[Strategy: The Choice of Leastness Method]
Because this corollary is a direct algebraic consequence of the Archimedean Property, we prove it through simple variable transformation ($x = 1/\varepsilon$) rather than using the supremum's $\varepsilon$-characterization directly. In the hierarchy of your repository, the Archimedean Property (which relies on "leastness") does the heavy lifting, allowing the corollary to be a "Side A" algebraic derivation.
\end{remark*}

\begin{remark*}[Proof structure]
Choose the real threshold $y/x$, use the Archimedean Property to find a
natural number above that threshold, and then rescale by the positive number
$x$.
\end{remark*}
