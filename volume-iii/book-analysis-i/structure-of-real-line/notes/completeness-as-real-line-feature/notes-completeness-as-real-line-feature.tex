\begin{topicbox}{Completeness as the New Real-Line Feature}
\begin{exposition}
The decisive new feature of \(\mathbb R\) is completeness. The rationals have
many algebraic and order properties, but they do not contain all limits
suggested by rational approximation processes. The real-number construction
adds enough points so that the order has the completeness behavior required
for analysis.
\end{exposition}

\begin{exposition}
This chapter does not introduce new completeness principles beyond those
already developed in the volume. Its role is to gather the picture: the real
line is an ordered field containing embedded copies of the earlier number
systems, and its completeness is what makes it the natural setting for the
next volume's analytic arguments.
\end{exposition}
\end{topicbox}
