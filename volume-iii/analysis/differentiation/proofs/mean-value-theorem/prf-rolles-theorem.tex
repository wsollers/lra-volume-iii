\begin{theorem*}[Rolle's Theorem]
Let $a, b \in \mathbb{R}$ with $a < b$. Suppose that $f : [a,b] \to \mathbb{R}$ is continuous on $[a,b]$, differentiable on $(a,b)$, and satisfies $f(a) = f(b)$. Then there exists $c \in (a,b)$ such that $f'(c) = 0$.
\end{theorem*}
