\begin{remark*}[Structural Roadmap]
This chapter establishes the precise analytic definitions and
fundamental limit properties of the classical elementary functions.
The exponential $e^x$ is defined by its absolutely convergent power
series; the natural logarithm $\ln x$ as its inverse (or
equivalently as $\int_1^x 1/t\, dt$); real powers $x^r$ via
$e^{r \ln x}$. The trigonometric functions are defined by their
power series and characterized by the Pythagorean identity, addition
formulas, and the fundamental limit $\sin x / x \to 1$. Hyperbolic
functions are defined as even and odd parts of $e^x$.

The chapter depends on: absolute convergence of power series,
continuity of functions defined by convergent series, and the
algebra of limits. It feeds into: differentiation rules for all
standard functions, Taylor series theory, and integration.
\end{remark*}
