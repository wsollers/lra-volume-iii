\begin{lemma*}[Straddle Lemma]
If \(F\) is differentiable at \(\xi\) with \(F'(\xi)=f(\xi)\), then the
one-slab linearization error across any sufficiently small interval straddling
\(\xi\) is bounded by \(\varepsilon\) times its length.
\end{lemma*}
\begin{proof}[Professional Standard Proof]
\LRAProofBodyStart
By differentiability, choose \(\delta(\xi)>0\) such that
\[
  |F(t)-F(\xi)-f(\xi)(t-\xi)|\le \varepsilon |t-\xi|
\]
whenever \(|t-\xi|<\delta(\xi)\). Apply this once at \(t=v\) and once at
\(t=u\). Since
\[
  F(v)-F(u)=\bigl(F(v)-F(\xi)\bigr)-\bigl(F(u)-F(\xi)\bigr),
\]
the triangle inequality gives
\[
  |F(v)-F(u)-f(\xi)(v-u)|
  \le \varepsilon(v-\xi)+\varepsilon(\xi-u)
  =\varepsilon(v-u).
\]
\end{proof}
\begin{proof}[Detailed Learning Proof]
\LRAProofBodyStart
Differentiability says that near \(\xi\), the increment from \(\xi\) to any
nearby point is well approximated by the linear term \(f(\xi)(t-\xi)\). Apply
that estimate to the right endpoint \(v\) and the left endpoint \(u\). The
errors add, and the distances \((v-\xi)\) and \((\xi-u)\) add to \(v-u\).
\end{proof}
\begin{remark*}[Proof structure]
Apply the pointwise differentiability estimate at both ends of a tagged slab
and subtract the two linear approximations.
\end{remark*}
