\begin{topicbox}{Density and No Adjacent Points}
\begin{exposition}
The rational numbers are already densely ordered: between two distinct
rational numbers there is another rational number. The real line retains
this no-adjacent-points behavior. Between two distinct real numbers there is
room for intermediate points, and rational points sit densely inside the real
line once the rational embedding has been fixed.
\end{exposition}

\begin{exposition}
Density should not be confused with completeness. The rationals are dense in
themselves and dense in the reals, but they still fail to contain all limits
that real analysis needs. The real line adds the missing completeness while
keeping the ordered-field behavior already visible in \(\mathbb Q\).
\end{exposition}
\end{topicbox}
