\begin{theorem*}[L'H\^{o}pital's Rule, \(0/0\) Form]
If \(f,g\) are differentiable near \(a^+\), \(g'\neq 0\), \(f(x)\to 0\), \(g(x)\to 0\), and \(f'(x)/g'(x)\to L\), then \(f(x)/g(x)\to L\).
\end{theorem*}

\begin{proof}[Professional Standard Proof]
\LRAProofBodyStart
Assume first that \(L\in\mathbb{R}\). Extend \(f\) and \(g\) to \(a\) by defining \(f(a)=g(a)=0\). The hypotheses make these extensions continuous at \(a\). For \(x\in(a,b)\), the Cauchy Mean Value Theorem on \([a,x]\) gives a point \(\xi_x\in(a,x)\) such that
\[
  \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}
  =
  \frac{f(x)-f(a)}{g(x)-g(a)}
  =
  \frac{f'(\xi_x)}{g'(\xi_x)}.
\]
The denominator \(g(x)\) is nonzero for \(x\) sufficiently close to \(a\); otherwise Rolle's Theorem would force a zero of \(g'\) between \(a\) and \(x\). Since \(a<\xi_x<x\), the squeeze theorem gives \(\xi_x\to a^+\) as \(x\to a^+\). Therefore \(f'(\xi_x)/g'(\xi_x)\to L\), and hence \(f(x)/g(x)\to L\).

For \(L=+\infty\) or \(L=-\infty\), the same argument replaces the \(\varepsilon\)-estimate by the corresponding eventual lower or upper bound.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Detailed Learning Proof]
\LRAProofBodyStart
The \(0/0\) hypothesis allows the endpoint values to be filled in as \(f(a)=g(a)=0\). Cauchy's theorem then says that the quotient of endpoint changes equals a derivative quotient somewhere between the endpoints. As \(x\) moves toward \(a\), that intermediate point is trapped between \(a\) and \(x\), so it also moves toward \(a\). The assumed derivative-ratio limit transfers directly to the original quotient.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark*}[Proof structure]
Fill in the endpoint, apply Cauchy MVT on \([a,x]\), and squeeze the intermediate point toward \(a\).
\end{remark*}
