\section{Modulus}

\begin{toolkitbox}{Toolkit: Modulus}
The absolute value (modulus) of a real number is the fundamental measure
of its magnitude, stripped of sign.  The atomic notions formalised below
are:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{absolute value} --- the piecewise definition
        $\lvert a \rvert := \max(a, -a)$;
  \item \textbf{nonnegativity} --- $\lvert a \rvert \ge 0$ for all
        $a \in \mathbb{R}$;
  \item \textbf{zero characterisation} --- $\lvert a \rvert = 0$ if and
        only if $a = 0$;
  \item \textbf{self-or-negation} --- $\lvert a \rvert$ equals either $a$
        or $-a$;
  \item \textbf{symmetry} --- $\lvert -a \rvert = \lvert a \rvert$;
  \item \textbf{multiplicativity} --- $\lvert ab \rvert =
        \lvert a \rvert \lvert b \rvert$;
  \item \textbf{quotient rule} --- $\lvert a/b \rvert =
        \lvert a \rvert / \lvert b \rvert$ for $b \ne 0$;
  \item \textbf{bound by modulus} ---
        $-\lvert a \rvert \le a \le \lvert a \rvert$;
  \item \textbf{nonstrict modulus inequality} ---
        $\lvert a \rvert \le r \iff -r \le a \le r$;
  \item \textbf{strict modulus inequality} ---
        $\lvert a \rvert < r \iff -r < a < r$;
  \item \textbf{triangle inequality} ---
        $\lvert a + b \rvert \le \lvert a \rvert + \lvert b \rvert$;
  \item \textbf{reverse triangle inequality} ---
        $\lvert \lvert a \rvert - \lvert b \rvert \rvert
        \le \lvert a - b \rvert$;
  \item \textbf{generalised triangle inequality} ---
        $\lvert \sum_{k=1}^{n} a_k \rvert
        \le \sum_{k=1}^{n} \lvert a_k \rvert$.
\end{itemize}
\end{toolkitbox}
