\captionof{figure}{%
  Three steps of the bisection proof of the Intermediate Value Theorem
  (\cref{prop:ivt-bisection}) for $f(a)<0<f(b)$.
  At each step the half preserving a sign change is retained and the other
  discarded; lengths halve as $(b-a)/2^n \to 0$.
  By NIP, $c \in \bigcap I_n$ exists uniquely; continuity forces $f(c) = 0$.%
