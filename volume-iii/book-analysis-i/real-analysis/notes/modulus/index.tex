\midrule
\hyperref[def:absolute-value]{Absolute value} & Magnitude stripped of sign.\\
\hyperref[thm:absolute-value-nonneg]{Nonnegativity} & Modulus is always nonnegative.\\
\hyperref[thm:absolute-value-zero-iff-zero]{Zero characterisation} & Only $0$ has modulus $0$.\\
\hyperref[thm:absolute-value-self-or-neg]{Self or negation} & $|a|$ is either $a$ or $-a$.\\
\hyperref[thm:absolute-value-symmetric]{Symmetry} & Negation does not change magnitude.\\
\hyperref[thm:absolute-value-product]{Product rule} & Modulus is multiplicative.\\
\hyperref[thm:absolute-value-quotient]{Quotient rule} & Division respects modulus when the denominator is nonzero.\\
\hyperref[thm:absolute-value-bounds]{Bounds by modulus} & $a$ lies between $-|a|$ and $|a|$.\\
\hyperref[thm:absolute-value-le-iff]{Nonstrict modulus inequality} & $|a|\le r$ is equivalent to a two-sided bound.\\
\hyperref[thm:absolute-value-lt-iff]{Strict modulus inequality} & $|a|<r$ is equivalent to a strict two-sided bound.\\
\hyperref[thm:reverse-triangle-inequality]{Reverse triangle inequality} & Magnitudes vary no faster than distance.\\
\hyperref[thm:absolute-value-sum-bound]{Finite triangle inequality} & The modulus of a finite sum is bounded by summed moduli.\\
