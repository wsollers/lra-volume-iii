\begin{theorem*}[Mean Value Theorem]
Let $a, b \in \mathbb{R}$ with $a < b$. Suppose that $f : [a,b] \to \mathbb{R}$ is continuous on $[a,b]$ and differentiable on $(a,b)$. Then there exists $c \in (a,b)$ such that $f'(c) = \dfrac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}$.
\end{theorem*}
