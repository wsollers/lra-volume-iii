\section*{Roadmap}

This chapter formalizes the arithmetic structure of the integers and the
natural divisibility phenomena that arise from it: divisibility, primes,
greatest common divisors, congruences, and the first structural theorems of
elementary number theory.

\textbf{Depends on:} Measure Theory ---
the prior analysis sequence completes the foundational treatment of limits,
integration, and measurable structure before the volume turns toward the
algebraic and arithmetic organization of discrete mathematical objects.

\textbf{Feeds into:} Algebraic Structures ---
elementary number theory supplies concrete examples of binary operations,
algebraic laws, quotients modulo an equivalence relation, and arithmetic
structures that motivate later abstract algebra.
