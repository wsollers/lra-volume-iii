\subsection{From Characterization to Computation}

\begin{remark*}[Interpretation]
Carath\'{e}odory's characterization turns differentiability into continuity of a slope factor. If
\[
  f(x)-f(c)=(x-c)\phi_f(x),
  \qquad
  g(x)-g(c)=(x-c)\phi_g(x),
\]
with $\phi_f$ and $\phi_g$ continuous at $c$, then the standard algebraic rules for derivatives follow from the algebra of continuous functions. The recurring move is no longer to manipulate a punctured quotient directly, but to construct a continuous factor whose value at $c$ is the derivative.
\end{remark*}
