\begin{theorem*}[Polynomial detection]
Finite differences detect polynomial degree.
\end{theorem*}

\begin{proof}[Professional Standard Proof]
\LRAProofBodyStart
Write \(f(n) = a_d n^d + a_{d-1}n^{d-1} + \cdots + a_0\) with \(a_d \ne 0\).
We show by induction on \(d\) that \(\Delta f\) is a polynomial of degree
\(d-1\) with leading coefficient \(d\,a_d\).

\emph{Base case} (\(d = 0\)): a constant polynomial has \(\Delta f = 0\). \(\checkmark\)

\emph{Inductive step}: Assume the result holds for all polynomials of degree
less than \(d\). Then
\[
\Delta f(n) = f(n+1)-f(n)
= a_d\bigl((n+1)^d - n^d\bigr) + \text{(lower-degree terms)}.
\]
Expanding \((n+1)^d - n^d = d\,n^{d-1} + \binom{d}{2}n^{d-2} + \cdots\),
the leading term is \(d\,a_d\,n^{d-1}\). Thus \(\Delta f\) is a polynomial
of degree \(d-1\) with leading coefficient \(d\,a_d \ne 0\).

By induction, applying \(\Delta\) a further \(d-1\) times yields the
constant \(\Delta^d f(n) = d!\,a_d\), and one further application gives \(0\).
\end{proof}
