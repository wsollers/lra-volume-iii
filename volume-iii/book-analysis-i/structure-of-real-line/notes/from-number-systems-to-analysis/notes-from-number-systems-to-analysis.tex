\begin{topicbox}{From Number Systems to Analysis}
\begin{exposition}
The construction of the number systems ends with a line that supports the
ordinary algebra of calculation, the order language of inequalities and
intervals, and the completeness needed to control limiting processes. This is
why the real line is not merely another extension in the chain. It is the
first system in the volume that has the full structural package needed for
real analysis.
\end{exposition}

\begin{exposition}
The formalization chapter that follows looks back at the foundational
construction rather than adding more real-line theory. The purpose of the
present chapter is to close the mathematical narrative: the earlier systems
are embedded into one another, and the final ordered field provides the
setting in which the rest of analysis can proceed.
\end{exposition}
\end{topicbox}
