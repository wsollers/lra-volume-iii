\begin{topicbox}{Ordered Field Structure}
\begin{exposition}
The real line combines algebra and order. As a field, it supports addition,
subtraction, multiplication, and division by nonzero elements. As an ordered
field, these operations interact with the order in the expected way: adding
the same quantity preserves order, and multiplying by a positive quantity
preserves order. These properties are inherited from the real-number
construction and the ordered-field structure developed earlier.
\end{exposition}
\end{topicbox}
